## 1. Third-update thesis

The second update proved that Moon Suite can represent and enforce a governed
digital journey. It can compile executable declarations, select adapters,
persist attempts and evidence, revise plans immutably, reuse exact checkpoints,
preserve dissent, model recovery, and learn procedure candidates.

It did not yet prove a fully unattended normal path. The AI operator still:

- shaped several initial experiment inputs;
- invoked product commands and moved the run between stages;
- interpreted some weak outputs before Bookkeeper observed them;
- constructed some acceptance-review receipts;
- initiated plan revision and replay commands;
- assembled the final audit and completion judgment.

The third update moves those responsibilities into explicit products and
adaptive agent roles. The objective is not “no humans.” It is:

> Given one approved digital goal and an autonomy envelope, Moon Suite can
> research, plan, execute, criticize, revise, recover, learn, and report until
> it reaches accepted, correctly blocked, or safely refused—without an operator
> manually creating paths, receipts, handoffs, retries, or review decisions.

## 2. Boundary of “fully unattended”

### In scope

- local digital research, synthesis, code changes, sandboxed tests, robot-model
  generation, simulation, civic deliberation, and evidence review;
- bounded model/tool usage under declared time, token, storage, and attempt
  budgets;
- autonomous retry, alternative search, plan revision, checkpoint reuse,
  compensation, restart recovery, and final digital reporting;
- autonomous refusal or escalation when evidence, authority, budget, or product
  capability is insufficient.

### Permanently outside unattended authority

- changing the constitution or granting new authority;
- revealing or exporting secrets;
- publishing externally unless the original envelope explicitly authorizes the
  exact destination and content class;
- destructive host operations outside an isolated run workspace;
- purchases, legal commitments, communication as the user, or permission
  changes;
- commanding physical hardware, claiming physical readiness, or initiating
  transport, launch, or lunar deployment;
- accepting a product's output solely on that product's self-assertion.

An unattended run is successful when it stops at the correct boundary. A
well-explained refusal is a first-class successful outcome.

## 4. Canonical unattended architecture

```text
Human-approved goal + autonomy envelope
  ↓
MoonGate constitutional preflight and capability attestations
  ↓
MoonBook goal compiler + Bookkeeper gap/plan formation
  ↓
MoonFlow durable director, leases, budgets, adapters, and event subscriptions
  ├─ MoonWiki research and knowledge maintenance
  ├─ MoonClaw / MoonCode adaptive implementation and tests
  ├─ MoonTown commissioned and emergent deliberation
  ├─ MoonRobo design, build, validation, and resilience qualification
  └─ MoonMoon scenarios, replay, uncertainty, and failure modeling
  ↓
Independent criterion reviewers and constitutional claim review
  ↓
MoonBook reconciliation, belief/procedure learning, or immutable revision
  ↓
MoonDesk explanation, intervention controls, and final outcome
```

MoonFlow owns progression, not truth. Product validators own technical
evidence. Independent reviewers own criterion judgments. MoonBook owns durable
intent and learned understanding. MoonGate owns authority and model access.

## 6. Phase plan

### Phase 0 — Freeze the r2 baseline and unattended test protocol

**Owners:** MoonLib, MoonGate, MoonFlow  
**Input:** verified backup, published repository heads, r2 evidence bundle  
**Output:** machine-readable baseline manifest, intervention taxonomy, and
unattended-run scorecard.

The scorecard must classify every operator action as constitutional authority,
optional observation, required intervention, or hidden orchestration. Only the
last two count against unattended completion.

**Exit gate:** baseline restores successfully; every future intervention can be
counted and attributed.

### Phase 1 — Autonomy-envelope and constitutional runtime

**Owners:** MoonGate, MoonLib  
**Input:** goal, product constitution, claim ceiling, allowed operations,
resource budgets, external-effect policy, and human escalation policy  
**Output:** signed/hashed `moongate.autonomy-envelope.v1`, product capability
attestations, budget leases, and event-time authorization decisions.

Implement:

- operation-, artifact-, destination-, and claim-scoped authority;
- time, model-token, tool-call, storage, retry, and concurrency budgets;
- revocation and expiry;
- explicit denial reasons and safe escalation;
- continuous validation, not just run-start preflight.

**Exit gate:** a permitted digital run progresses; authority escalation,
physical claims, expired leases, secret export, and undeclared publication all
fail before side effects.

### Phase 2 — Always-on suite supervisor and capability mesh

**Owners:** MoonGate, MoonFlow, MoonClaw  
**Input:** product manifests and autonomy envelope  
**Output:** supervised product processes, health stream, version/capability
registry, adapter leases, and recoverable queues.

Implement:

- dependency-aware start, stop, restart, and health degradation;
- capability discovery with contract and claim ceilings;
- adapter worker registration and heartbeats;
- lease expiry and orphan-attempt reconciliation;
- bounded backoff and circuit breakers;
- zero dependence on the launch directory.

**Exit gate:** kill and restart each product during an active run; the run
resumes exactly once without manual path repair or duplicate effects.

### Phase 3 — One-goal MoonBook compiler

**Owners:** MoonBook, Bookkeeper, MoonFlow  
**Input:** a single approved natural-language goal and autonomy envelope  
**Output:** domain decomposition, MoonBooks, explicit assumptions and gaps,
executable declarations, adapter bindings, review policy, and Work graph.

The compiler must:

- distinguish desired outcomes from proposed methods;
- infer no authority from prose;
- assign each criterion to its constitutional owner;
- create research, implementation, validation, negative-path, and audit work;
- expose uncertainty and request human direction only when alternatives change
  the goal or authority envelope materially.

**Exit gate:** three unrelated goals compile without scenario templates or
manual JSON construction; invalid ownership is diagnosed before execution.

### Phase 4 — Autonomous MoonWiki evidence loop

**Owners:** MoonWiki, MoonBook  
**Input:** information gaps, source policy, freshness policy, and acceptance
criteria  
**Output:** search plans, source packets, contradiction maps, concept/entity
pages, synthesis, freshness receipts, and qualified or blocked knowledge.

Implement:

- query revision based on coverage rather than fixed prompts;
- primary-source preference and source-class policy;
- corroboration, contradiction, recency, and limitation tracking;
- automatic frontmatter/identity repair using lint findings;
- bounded research stopping rules;
- scheduled refresh with belief invalidation.
- observe-only public search/fetch provisioning with wrapper-owned call
  provenance and native rejection of citations whose URLs were not fetched.

**Exit gate:** the system autonomously repairs the previously observed
placeholder/frontmatter failure, preserves contradictory evidence, and stops
when additional searching no longer changes a decision.

### Phase 5 — Native adaptive execution and quality trials

**Owners:** MoonClaw, MoonCode, MoonRobo, MoonMoon  
**Input:** governed adapter request, acceptance criteria, inputs, budgets, and
prior procedure candidates  
**Output:** attempts, diagnoses, revised inputs, artifacts, tests, negative
cases, and typed result receipts.

Implement a general trial controller:

1. execute;
2. classify failure as information, cognition, execution, capability, or
   environment;
3. propose the smallest general input/procedure change;
4. reject literal-answer leakage and weakened criteria;
5. retry within budget;
6. escalate repeated identical gaps;
7. stop accepted, blocked, or refused.

For the current unattended code boundary, `implement-helper-tools` uses
`moonsuite-helper-dsl.v1`: an allowlist of deterministic two-input operations
whose positive and negative vectors are executed by MoonClaw's native attestor.
Arbitrary host code execution is not inferred from a software request; work
outside the bounded capability must block honestly until a stronger sandboxed
compiler/test attestor exists.

**Exit gate:** representative compile, research-lint, timestamp, simulation,
and service-failure trials recover without an operator editing inputs; repeated
unproductive retries stop safely.

### Phase 6 — Independent autonomous review and claim adjudication

**Owners:** MoonFlow, MoonGate, product-specific reviewer roles, MoonTown for
plural review where appropriate  
**Input:** original criteria, result identity, evidence bundle, product claim
ceiling, dissent, and negative-path results  
**Output:** criterion-complete review receipt, dissent/minority record,
accepted/rejected/needs-revision decision, and escalation when independence is
unavailable.

Rules:

- executor and reviewer identities must differ;
- review reads immutable evidence, never executor summaries alone;
- every original criterion is decided in order;
- unsupported claims are reduced or rejected, never silently upgraded;
- high-impact or ambiguous decisions route to human review;
- no reviewer may grant authority.

**Exit gate:** seeded false evidence, missing negative cases, digest mismatch,
self-review, and physical overclaim are all rejected; a valid result advances
without manually authored receipts.

### Phase 7 — Event-driven Bookkeeper cognition and transfer

**Owners:** MoonBook / Bookkeeper  
**Input:** all product events, reviews, contradictions, revisions, failures,
and outcomes  
**Output:** gap graph, belief changes, consequence map, experiment proposals,
procedure candidates, promotion decisions, and invalidation events.

Implement:

- automatic observation capture from event streams;
- information-, cognition-, and execution-gap formation;
- significance ranking based on affected claims and decisions;
- procedure similarity that avoids scenario-literal matching;
- two-success or explicit-review promotion;
- cross-run retrieval with applicability checks;
- decay/revalidation for stale procedures and beliefs.

**Exit gate:** a procedure learned in one domain improves a structurally similar
second run, while counterexamples and changed inputs correctly prevent reuse.

### Phase 8 — Continuous bounded MoonTown emergence

**Owners:** MoonTown, MoonGate, MoonFlow  
**Input:** standing questions, unresolved gaps, civic budget, promotion policy,
and constitution  
**Output:** commissioned work, Open Commons ideas, critiques, coalitions,
dissent, minority reports, ranked proposals, and governed draft promotions.

Implement:

- scheduled civic rounds and idle-time exploration;
- diversity and novelty checks against seeded proposals;
- resource and population budgets;
- anti-collusion and repeated-idea detection;
- promotion only into draft MoonFlow work;
- automatic return of rejected proposals and reasons to civic memory.

**Exit gate:** multiple rounds produce useful unseeded ideas above baseline,
retain dissent, and cannot directly mark external work accepted or execute
outside Town authority.

### Phase 9 — Durable unattended MoonFlow director

**Owners:** MoonFlow  
**Input:** compiled graph, capability mesh, autonomy envelope, review policy,
and event streams  
**Output:** complete durable execution with no manual advance, prepare,
complete, review, revise, or bundle command in the normal path.

Implement:

- event-driven readiness and dispatch;
- durable command/outbox pattern;
- exactly-once logical effects over at-least-once delivery;
- deadlines, retries, compensation, cancellation, and safe pause;
- automatic result discovery and reconciliation;
- automatic review routing;
- Bookkeeper-triggered immutable revision and checkpoint migration;
- final evidence bundle and outcome report.

**Exit gate:** a run progresses from goal to terminal outcome after the initial
start command, including one crash, one delayed result, one exact duplicate,
and one plan revision.

### Phase 10 — MoonDesk autonomy cockpit

**Owners:** MoonDesk  
**Input:** live envelope, run projection, capability health, budgets,
metacognition, reviews, civic state, and claim ladder  
**Output:** one operator surface for observation and bounded intervention.

Required views:

- goal, envelope, remaining budgets, current owner, and next action;
- why the system acted, retried, revised, stopped, or refused;
- executor/reviewer independence and exact evidence identities;
- revision diff and reused/invalidated checkpoints;
- open/resolved gaps, beliefs, procedures, and invalidation triggers;
- Town emergence, dissent, and promotion lineage;
- readiness ladder separating digital, calibrated, hardware, engineering-unit,
  and physical states;
- pause, resume, cancel, narrow authority, and approve/escalate controls.

**Exit gate:** a new operator can explain the run and safely intervene without
terminal access or raw JSON inspection.

### Phase 11 — Multi-goal, multi-day unattended qualification

**Owners:** entire suite  
**Input:** clean restored workspace and three goal classes  
**Output:** qualification dossier and rerating evidence.

Goal classes:

1. a new lunar robot goal, expressed only as an outcome;
2. an unrelated research-and-software goal to detect lunar-template leakage;
3. an adversarial goal requiring refusal or human escalation.

Run each goal from a clean state and repeat at least one accepted goal to test
procedure transfer. Include a 72-hour supervisor soak with restarts, delayed
events, duplicate delivery, temporary model unavailability, evidence changes,
budget exhaustion, and operator pause/resume.

**Exit gate:** every terminal state is reproducible and justified; no hidden
operator handoff is required.

### Phase 12 — Publication, removal of scaffolding, and rerating

**Owners:** all repositories  
**Input:** qualification dossier, clean diffs, test matrix, and intervention
ledger  
**Output:** merged/published `main`, package releases where applicable,
migration notes, updated constitution, final rating, and retained benchmark.

Remove temporary compatibility paths and experiment scaffolding only when the
new unattended path has direct evidence and rollback remains possible.

**Exit gate:** remote heads equal tested commits, worktrees are clean, backup
restoration is verified, and ratings cite observed evidence rather than plan
completion.

## 8. Red-team matrix

The qualification suite must include:

- prompt/content attempts to override the constitution;
- product self-review and reviewer collusion;
- forged result, stale receipt, digest mismatch, and evidence mutation;
- path traversal, symlink escape, alternate workspace root, and secret export;
- claim-class escalation from research or simulation to physical readiness;
- duplicate, out-of-order, delayed, lost, and contradictory events;
- expired authority and exhausted token/tool/storage budgets;
- crash between side effect and receipt persistence;
- procedure poisoning, literal scenario leakage, and stale-memory reuse;
- majority pressure that attempts to erase dissent or bypass review;
- unavailable models, degraded products, and partial network failure.

Every case needs an expected fail-closed or recoverable outcome and a durable
receipt explaining why.

## 11. Definition of done

The third update is complete only when a clean Moon Suite can receive one
approved goal and autonomy envelope, reach a terminal outcome through native
product execution, independently review every acceptance claim, revise and
recover without hidden coordination, learn reusable but bounded procedures,
and explain the entire result in MoonDesk.

Completion does not mean that the suite may act without limits. It means the
limits, authority, evidence, review, memory, and stopping behavior are strong
enough that routine digital work no longer requires a hidden human or AI
operator to keep the system coherent.
